# Section 28 — Consent known to be given under fear or misconception.

**Act:** Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita, 2023  
**Chapter:** 03 — General Exceptions

---

IPC [Section 90 
 A consent](../sections/section-90.md) is not such a consent as is intended by any section of this Sanhita, 
 if the consent is given by a person under fear of injury, or under a misconception of fact, and if the person doing the act knows, or has reason to believe, that the consent was given in consequence of such fear or misconception; or 
 if the consent is given by a person who, from mental illness, or intoxication, is unable to understand the nature and consequence of that to which he gives his consent; or 
 unless the contrary appears from the context, if the consent is given by a person who is under twelve years of age.
